Identity, values and Strengths

  • Ethical decision.

    • A situation where I had to make an ethical decision was when I picked up a patient from the hospital to the rest home.
    As my colleague and I arrived at the rest home and took the patient back to the room, we noticed that the bed was not made.
    Staff told us to transfer the patient onto the bed. My colleague and I looked at each other in shock and told them we are not going to unless the bed is made.
    After we had same conversation back and forth the staff finally decided to make a bed. I weighed values of equality, fairness and respect in this situation.
    No matter what situation the patient is in, that is not a reason to treat anyone like the staff did.
    As a result of us refusing, we were able to get the patient what they deserve as any patients should do.

  • How your culture has influenced your values and identity.

    • To understand myself better I will give a little background of myself.
    I was born in Japan. As I was growing up in Japan, respect was a fundamental part of life.
    This applied not just to people but everything like the way I speak or say things, food, toys to give an idea. The culture also influenced me to be reserved.
    After moving to New Zealand at young age, the cultural differences influenced how I think which shaped me to be more open and outspoken.
    Furthermore, the ability to understand two different cultures and the experiences benefited me to be more understanding and non judgemental.

  • Experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension.
    Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now, and why.

    • This experience was when I had to work with 5 other people in a group. The task was to pick a project as group, do research and write a paragraph each.
    We all started off fine then Covid hit so we had to work remotely. We set up a meeting every week for a couple of hours to see where we are at.
    After a couple of weeks, I have noticed that one of them was not attending the meeting. I have left him a message see what is happening but there was no reply. In the next meeting I asked the team if they knew anything whom also heard nothing.
    I suggested the team to leave him a message. Because I was not sure why he was not cooperating and if the person needed help, we were there to help. I also wanted the team to get involved so that it was not just me trying to find out what is happening but as a team.
    After a few attempts as team, he replied.
    I think it did work fine even it took us a few attempts because it was the only communication tool we had to reach out at the time.
    If I am in a similar situation again, I would make sure I have more than one communication tools. and if this is in work environment, I will talk to manager/leader see if they can find out what is happening.
    Because whatever reason the person has, he/she might not be comfortable talking to colleagues and I want to respect that personal space.
