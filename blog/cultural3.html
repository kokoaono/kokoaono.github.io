EQ/IQ-Emotional Intelligence

  • What is Emotional Intelligence
    • Emotional intelligence is the ability to perceive, control and evaluate emotions.
    The ability to understand, interpret and respond to the emotions of others as well as the ability to express and control one’s own emotions.
    There are five key components which are self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy and social skills.
    Being self-aware means that individual knows what emotions the person is feeling. Self-regulation is the ability to regulate individuals effectively.
    The ability to stay in control, not letting emotions to make decisions or verbally attack others.
    Motivation/self-motivated individuals will work towards their goal consistently.
    The ability to find a good thing or two even in a failure. Empathy, the ability to see yourself in someone else’s situation.
    Lastly, social skills are the ability to communicate well and able to set an example.

  • How is it different to IQ?
    • IQ is intellectual abilities. It reflects cognitive skills such as reasoning, logic and problem-solving.
    Whereas EQ is the ability to sense, respond, and interpret emotions effectively.

  • Why is it important?
    • Emotional intelligence allows to help, understand and manage individuals’ emotions in order to build positive social interactions.
    Especially at workplace where different personalities, skills and emotions come together, the ability to navigate one’s own emotions effectively and appropriately is an essential skill.

  • My understanding of Emotional Intelligence
    • My understanding of emotional intelligence is the ability to understand, control and express your own emotions effectively to build a healthy relationship with others as well as improve yourself.
    The key elements are self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy and social skills.
    Become more aware of how individual feels and be able to acknowledge, evaluate and deal with it rather than acting or deciding based on how one feels is a significant part of emotional intelligence.
    This gives an opportunity to reflect on your feelings and yourself in general. Also teaches us to be more rational and focus on things that is important to you in day to day life.
